How To Make A Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

Jollof Rice is one of the most popular dishes in West Africa, especially in Nigeria. It is a flavorful one-pot rice dish cooked in a rich tomato-based sauce with spices and seasonings.
It is often served at parties, family gatherings, and special occasions.

Recipe Details:

  • Preparation Time: 20 Minutes
  • Cook Time: 45 Minutes
  • Servings: 4-6 People
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of any rice of your choice
  • 10 medium sized tomatoes
  • 5 red bell peppers
  • 4 scotch bonnet peppers (popularly known as "Ata Rodo or Rodo")
  • 2 large onion
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable/groundnut oil
  • 3 tablespoons of tomatoe paste
  • 2 cups of beef stock (or any protein of your choice)
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Moderate amount of salt based on the taste
  • 500g of any protein of choice

Instructions:

  1. Wash and blend the tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and one onion until smooth.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pot, then fry the chopped onion for 2 minutes.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for another 3 minutes.
  4. Pour in the blended pepper mixture and cook until the sauce thickens and the water reduces.
  5. Add protein stock, seasoning cubes, thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, and salt. Stir well.
  6. Wash the rice and add it into the sauce. Stir until evenly coated.
  7. Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat for 25 to 30 minutes until the rice is tender.
  8. Stir occasionally to prevent burning, and add a little water if necessary.
  9. Serve hot with fried plantains, salad, or any grilled protein.

Additional Tips:

  • For the best smoky flavor, allow the rice to cook on low heat without stirring too often.
  • Use fresh tomatoes and peppers for a richer taste.
  • Avoid adding too much water so the rice does not become soggy.

Nutritional Facts:

  • Calories: 450 per serving
  • Protein: 15g per serving
  • Carbohydrates: 65g per serving
  • Fat: 8g per serving

Recipe Source:

This recipe is a traditional Nigerian Jollof Rice recipe passed down through generations.
